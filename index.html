About WCCAS

Established in 2013, WCCAS aims to produce globally competitive students prepared to meet the demands of today's workforce.

West Central College of Arts and Science is an educational institution founded by Mrs. Amorwena C. Langit and Alfred Langit with the belief that education has a remarkable effect on one's personality, transforming a person to live a better life.

We are fully accredited and recognized by the Commission on Higher Education (CHED), Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA), and Department of Education (DepEd) in the Philippines.

Frequently Asked Questions

Enrollment & Academic Records

Q: What should I do if I miss the official enrollment period?

You must visit the Registrar's Office immediately. Late enrollment may be allowed only up to two (2) weeks after classes begin, subject to approval and payment of applicable penalty fees.

Q: How do I request academic documents?

Submit a request form at the Registrar's Office and pay the applicable processing fee at the Cashier. Processing time usually takes 3 to 5 working days.

Q: Can I change or drop a subject after enrollment?

Yes, you may file a Subject Drop Form within the first 6 weeks of the semester, with valid reasons and necessary approvals.

Student Discipline & Conduct

Q: What should I do if I witness or experience bullying?

Report immediately to the Office of Student Affairs or Guidance Office. Your report will be treated confidentially and investigated promptly.

Q: Can I get suspended for not wearing the proper uniform?

Not on first offense. However, repeated violations may lead to disciplinary action. Cross-dressing in uniform is allowed in accordance with our inclusion policy.

Campus Life & Activities

Q: How can I join a student organization?

Attend orientation activities and sign up during recruitment week. You can join more than one organization, provided you manage your time well.

Q: Are students required to attend institutional events?

Yes, student participation in key institutional activities is mandatory unless excused for valid reasons. These events are part of holistic development.

Emergency Procedures

Fire Emergency

  • Alert others by shouting "Fire!" and activate the nearest fire alarm
  • Evacuate immediately - do not retrieve personal belongings
  • Use the nearest exit and proceed to the designated assembly area
  • If smoke is present, stay low and cover your nose and mouth
  • Report to your class adviser for attendance checking
  • Do not re-enter the building unless cleared by authorities

Earthquake Procedure

  • DROP to the ground
  • COVER under a sturdy desk or table
  • HOLD ON until the shaking stops
  • Stay away from glass, windows, or objects that may fall
  • If outdoors, move to an open area away from trees and power lines
  • After shaking stops, evacuate calmly and proceed to evacuation area
